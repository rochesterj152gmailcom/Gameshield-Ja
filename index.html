What Our Research Found

Real Threats Facing Jamaican Gamers

These are not hypothetical risks they are the threats our survey participants reported experiencing in Jamaica.

🎣

Phishing Scams

Fake login pages, fake giveaways, and scam messages designed to steal your credentials. One in four gamers surveyed has been targeted.

25.2% reported this
🔓

Account Hacking

Attackers use stolen or reused passwords to lock you out, drain virtual purchases, or impersonate you to scam your contacts.

12.6% reported this
💳

Unauthorized Purchases

Once your account is compromised, attackers can make in-game purchases using your linked payment methods before you notice.

🦠

Malicious Downloads

Unofficial mods, cracked games, and fake installers can contain spyware or keyloggers targeting your gaming credentials.

📱

Unsafe Mobile Apps

Unverified apps especially common in Jamaica's mobile-first gaming scene can expose your personal data and payment info through insecure permissions.

👤

Social Engineering

Attackers exploit gaming communities through fake streamers, DMs, and friend requests to manipulate you into handing over credentials.

From the Survey: Top Advice

What Jamaican Gamers Say Helps Most

When we asked respondents what they'd tell other local gamers, these were the most common pieces of advice ranked by frequency.

#1

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Mentioned by 34% of respondents. Even if your password is stolen, MFA adds a second barrier attackers can't easily bypass.

#2

Check Links Before You Click

Flagged by 27.8%. Look for odd domains, typos, fake urgency, or prize offers that seem too good to be true.

#3

Use Strong, Unique Passwords

Recommended by 25.8%. One compromised password should not unlock every account you own across gaming and email.

#4

Don't Share Account Details

Warned by 20.6%. Even with trusted people, sharing credentials weakens security and makes account recovery much harder.

#5

Use a Password Manager

Directly recommended by our research. Apps like Bitwarden or Google Password Manager let you create unique, strong passwords for every account without having to memorise them — eliminating the reuse habit entirely.

What Needs to Change

Jamaica's Betting, Gaming and Lotteries Commission (BGLC) has historically focused on financial compliance, leaving cybersecurity largely outside scope. Our study recommends sector-specific standards, a dedicated incident reporting mechanism, and digital literacy curricula in schools and universities.

In the meantime, if you've experienced a cyber incident while gaming, you can report it to the Jamaica Constabulary Force's Cybercrime Division — and use the form below.
